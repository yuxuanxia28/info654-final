The 1980s

The 1980s saw the development and proliferation of the goth aesthetic and lifestyle, based primarily on the music genre that emerged at this time. The style was widely influential, showing up in movies, fashion, makeup, and hairstyles that continue to entrance to this day.

Events of the 80s

AIDS Epidemic

Photo by Eric Luce

The AIDS Epidemic has killed 700,000 people as of 2018, a plague that disproportionately effected the queer community. The Reagan administration either ignored or openly mocked the thousands of people who were dying. This image shows the original San Francisco Gay Men's Chorus's surviving members in white coats. Their friends, lovers, and choirmates who died are represented by the people wearing black.

ACT UP

Image source: nyclgbtsites.org

ACT UP was a grassroots organization created in response to government inaction to address the ongoing crisis. They were instrumental in forcing the public to pay attention, stop the spread of misinformation, and change the hearts and minds of the public about the plight of queer people.

Fall of the Berlin Wall

Image source: app.abcnews.go.com

The Berlin Wall that separated East and West Germany and had long been a literal and symbolic division between the West and the Soviet Union came down on November 9, 1989. This marked the most significant decline in the Soviet Union before its eventual dissolution.

Professional Air Traffic Controllers Organization Strike

Image source: Jacobin.com

When Reagan brutally crushed the PATCO strike, forcing workers into unsafe working conditions, it marked a massive shift away from union power and organizing that would have ripple effects into the state and conditions of labor for decades.

Launch of MTV

Image source: The80sruled.com

The Music Television chanel launched on August 1, 1981 and quickly became an incredibly influential part of pop culture. The rise of the music video changed the way people consumed and discovered music.
